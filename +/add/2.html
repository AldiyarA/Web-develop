Recipe #1 Aam Panna

Ingredients Quantity
Green mangoes 4 medium-sized
Sugar 120ml
Rock salt 30ml
Roasted cumin seeds 5ml
2oasted chili 1
Fresh mint leaves Handful

Step 1:Cut mangoes into 3 slices each - two from either side of the seed and one with the seed in it.

Step 2:Pressure cook mangoes with one small cup of water. Turn off heat as soon as the pressure cooker whistles once.

Step 3:Open cooker after 5-7 minutes and leave to cool.

Step 4:Alternatively roast the whole mangoes over an open flame, till the skin gets chared and the flesh is soft. The charred skin will peel off easily and you can squeeze out the pulp with your hands. This gives a nice smoky flavour to the dish.

Step 5:Grind sugar and roasted cumin seeds using a food processor. (If using chilli, grind that too.)

Step 6:Mince mint leaves in a food processor.

Step 7:When cool, squeeze pulp of mangoes into a blender using hands. Discard skin and seeds.

Step 8:Purée mango pulp until smooth.

Step 9:Add ground sugar, rock salt, roasted cumin seeds, chilli powder and minced mint leaves. Blend well.

Step 10:Store in clean plastic/ glass bottle. Refrigerate.

Recipe #2 Custard Pie

Ingredients Quantity
Milk 2 cup
Flour 1/2
Baking powder 1/3 teaspoon
Salt 1 pinch
Soft butter 3 tablespoon
Large egg 4
Sugar 1/3 cup
Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon
Grated coconut 1

Step 1: Blend all ingredients for one minute in a blender.

Step 2: Pour into a buttered 9-inch pie plate.

Step 3: Bake 30 minutes at 400°F (200°C) (Gas Mark 6) or until a knife inserted one inch comes out clean.

Recipe #3 Louis Dressing

  • Category: Salad Dressing recipes
  • Time: 35 minutes
  • Serves: 6 (1/4 cup each)
Ingredients Quantity
Mayonnaise 1 cup
Mild salsa 1/4 cup
Ketchup 1/4 cup
Lemon juice 1 tablespoon
Worcestershire sauce 1 or 1/2 teaspoon
Hot sauce To your taste

Step 1:Combine all ingredients in a blender, and process till smooth. The goal is to have a pink smooth sauce with little green flecks.

Step 2:Pour the sauce into a bowl and chill covered in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes prior to use.
